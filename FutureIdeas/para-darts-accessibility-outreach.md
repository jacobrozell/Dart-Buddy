**Status:** Future idea — not scheduled. Capture for when accessibility work is ready for real-user review.

## What to ask them (when ready)

- Scoring flow at the oche with VoiceOver on (pad vs future voice/talk input)
- Whether score readout order and turn context make sense without sight
- Setup friction (roster, game options, Cricket board) before a match starts
- One-handed / seated / limited reach use cases common in para play
- What they use today (DartConnect, chalk, human caller) and what's missing

**Do not** lead with a product pitch. Offer free testing, no ads, local data, and honest "what's broken" feedback — same tone as local league outreach ([`docs/marketing/launch-strategy.md`](../docs/marketing/launch-strategy.md)).

---

## Outreach bar (before messaging)

1. Fix or document known a11y gaps from [`accessibility/accessibility_todo.md`](../accessibility/accessibility_todo.md)
2. Have 2–3 **specific questions**, not "is it accessible?"
3. Be ready to iterate from feedback (or explain what's fixed in a follow-up)
4. Optional: offer video call or async VoiceOver screen recording review

## Draft opener (for later)

> Hey — I'm the developer of Dart Buddy, a free no-ads iOS dart scorekeeper. I'm trying to make it work better for blind and low-vision players and para darts communities, and I don't want to guess at what actually helps at the board.
>
> Would anyone be open to trying it with VoiceOver (or however you usually score) and telling me what's confusing or missing? No pressure, no cost, everything stays on your phone.
